ABOUT

Hello, my name is Ben. I'm living in Tokyo teaching English right now. I'd like to work in web development in the future. I've completed a few full-stack projects. I also make indie pop music. And drink Stroh's. Check out some of my stuff below.

Jitenshark

This is a node.js web app that uses Google Maps API. Its purpose is to help people in Japan find good spots to park their bikes.

Badnname

This is another node.js web app, whose purpose is to help new bands find an unused bandname. It searches through different music websites to see if that name’s been taken.

Thenkyou

This is a Ruby/Sinatra app that was made for studying languages. It has a relational database that matches vocab terms with reading passages in different languages.

Bento Bento

I made this node.js-based blog for someone. It has an Admin login and functions to write, edit, and delete blog posts, as well as create other admins.

Sushi Backpack - Sour EP

February Records was kind enough to organize a digital release for the second EP of my indie pop group, Sushi Backpack. Gooey Fame (of Annabel) played drums, Nikki played bass, and Jen played keyboards.

Blithe Field - Days Drift By

My friend Spencer recorded this album of cool experimental music. I played cello on it. It was really fun to record, because I could play anything, knowing that Spencer would be able chop it up into something nice.

Spencer Radcliffe - Enjoy the Great Outdoors

Spencer also recorded this. We toured together in Fall 2017 playing some songs from this album. Good times!

Tampopo - Ben's Dad's Birthday EP

This is a split with one of my favorite bands, Tampopo (previously, The Glow Cats, as released on Jigsaw Records). Tampopo usually writes songs about food, so Kaori helped me write and record songs about okonomiyaki for this album.

Sushi Backpack - One Day You Have to Face Your Demos

This was my first release as Sushi Backpack. I recorded it on Bucky’s digital 8-track and then edited it in Final Cut Pro, because I was using that in my job as a video editor. Never mix music with FCPx!
